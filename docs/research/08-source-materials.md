# Source Materials: Bibliography and Tier 1 References

## Purpose

This document compiles key texts, papers, and sources for each school of thought's perspective on inflation. Priority is given to open-access and freely available materials where possible. These sources form the foundation for Tier 1 content (approved reference material) and inform Tier 2 content (platform-written explanations).

---

## Organization

Sources are organized by:
1. **School of thought**
2. **Type** (foundational texts, key papers, accessible introductions, data sources)
3. **Access** (open-access, paywalled, book)

## Recommended Tier 1 Documents for MVP

For the inflation module MVP, prioritize embedding/chunking these accessible and representative sources:

### One per school:

1. **Neoclassical**: Bank of England "Money Creation in the Modern Economy" (2014) + Mankiw textbook chapter on inflation
2. **Monetarist**: Friedman "The Counter-Revolution in Monetary Theory" (1970)
3. **Austrian**: Mises Institute introduction to ABCT; Hayek summary from "Prices and Production"
4. **Post-Keynesian**: Lavoie introduction to post-Keynesian inflation theory
5. **MMT**: Mosler "Seven Deadly Innocent Frauds" excerpts; Kelton "Deficit Myth" chapter on inflation
6. **Ecological**: Daly "Beyond Growth" excerpts; Jackson "Prosperity Without Growth" excerpts

### Cross-cutting:

7. Federal Reserve inflation data and definitions
8. Historical case study summaries (1970s, 2008, 2021-23)
